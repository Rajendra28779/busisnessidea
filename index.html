Renting Out Your Terrace: A Popular and Easy Way to Earn!

If you live in a city: You can rent your terrace for placing hoardings or banners. It's a great way to earn some extra income.

If you live in a village: You can rent your terrace for small towers or network installations.

You can earn anywhere from ₹25,000 to ₹50,000 per month with no investment!

Demand on market :-

Everyone Wants to Stabilize Their Business, and Advertising is Key!
One of the best ways to advertise is through hoardings and banners. Businesses use them to promote their products and services, which helps increase sales.
If your terrace has enough space, you can rent it out for hoardings and banners. This is an easy way for you to earn money while helping others advertise their business!

Incense stick ( अगर्बत्ती ) Cost : ₹50,000 - ₹1,00,000

Incense stick making is a popular medium-scale business due to its high returns with minimal effort. With an investment of ₹50,000 to ₹80,000, this business offers a great profit margin. The best part is that it requires no additional labor costs—one person can manage everything, from production and packaging to selling. Many companies provide machines for incense stick production and also buy back the finished product, eliminating the stress of finding buyers. This ensures a good profit range without any extra expenses!

Demand on market :-

Day by day, human needs are evolving, and the demand for incense sticks is steadily increasing. While they are traditionally used for puja and religious rituals, incense sticks are now widely used for mosquito repellent, meditation, and enhancing home ambiance. This growing demand across various sectors ensures a strong market. Additionally, local markets often have low competition, and the buy-back feature offered by many companies makes it easier to produce and sell products with a good profit margin.

Where to sell :-

The selling process for incense sticks is simple and efficient, especially with the buy-back feature. By purchasing the machine, you can sell the product back to the company, though this typically offers lower profit margins. For higher profits, consider selling directly in the local market and retail shops. Retailers can purchase your product at a slightly lower price and sell it to customers at market rates, ensuring consistent demand and turning them into your regular customers. Keep in mind that product quality should never be compromised, as this can negatively impact your sales.

Margin Calculation :-

Labor Cost: ₹400 (Per Day)

Production Rate: 1 kg produces 1000 to 1200 incense sticks

1 kg produces ~ 30 packets

1 box contains 12 packets, so 1 kg produces 2.5 boxes

If You Produce 10 kg per Day

A single person can produce 15 kg per day (machine is automated, and one person can guide the process)

If a person works dedicatedly, they can produce 20 kg per day, increasing the profit margin.

Drawbacks :-

While the incense stick business has many advantages, it also comes with a few challenges. Competing with established brands can be tough, and some retail shops may initially hesitate to accept your product. However, staying focused on sales and building trust with your quality will eventually win them over. Remember, maintaining high product quality is essential for earning customer loyalty and standing out in the market.

Prescription for Drawbacks :-

To overcome challenges in the incense stick business, always focus on delivering exceptional product quality. Ensure the fragrance is long-lasting and pleasant, as this greatly impacts customer satisfaction. Packaging also plays a crucial role in building trust—use strong, attractive, and high-quality packaging. Remember the saying, 'Jo dikhega, wohi bikega' (What looks appealing sells). Avoid marketing your product as cheap; instead, keep the pricing competitive with other brands to maintain value perception. For an added edge, include a scratch card inside the box as a special incentive for retailers. This small gesture not only makes your product more appealing but also encourages repeat purchases, boosting your sales effectively.

Conclusion :-

The incense stick business is a promising venture with low investment and high returns. By maintaining product quality, leveraging market demand, and implementing effective marketing strategies, you can establish a successful and sustainable business. With dedication and smart planning, this opportunity can bring significant profits and long-term growth.

Edible Cups: A New High-Demand Product
Edible cups are an innovative and eco-friendly product that is gaining significant demand in the market. With the right distribution strategies, these cups can quickly capture the market.
Tea plays a crucial role in our daily lives, and a tea seller in a market area can sell approximately 1,000 cups of tea per day. In college areas, this number can go up to 10,000 cups daily.
Some of the most popular tea brands, such as Chaiwala Babu, MBA Chai Wala, Mo Chai, Chai Sutta Bar, and Graduate Chaiwali, have gained fame for their unique innovations in tea offerings.
We can leverage their market presence and popularity by offering edible tea cups as a value-added product. By partnering with these established brands or targeting similar markets, edible cups can become a game-changer, contributing to a more sustainable and innovative tea culture.

Demand on market :-

Over the past decade, tea sellers have adapted their offerings based on customer demand and hygiene standards:
10 Years Ago: Tea was commonly served in reusable glass cups, costing around ₹5 per cup.
Rise of Paper Cups: As hygiene awareness grew, paper cups became the preferred choice for tea sellers, offering a disposable and clean solution.
Introduction of Matka Tea: To provide a traditional touch, matka (clay) cups were introduced. This trend gained popularity, especially among premium brands, with local tea costing ₹10 and branded tea reaching ₹20 per cup.
The demand for tea continues to increase daily, presenting a significant opportunity for innovation.
Edible tea cups combine sustainability, innovation, and novelty. With hygiene as a priority and consumer interest in unique experiences, these cups align perfectly with market needs. They offer Eco-friendliness: No waste, fully edible. Enhanced Experience: Adds flavor and fun to tea drinking. Eco-friendliness: No waste, fully edible. Appeal to Brands: Popular tea brands like MBA Chai Wala, Chai Sutta Bar, and others can integrate edible cups as a premium offering, further driving demand.
By introducing edible tea cups, we can tap into the ever-growing tea market and establish a new, exciting trend in the beverage industry.

Where to sell :-

The success of edible tea cups largely depends on choosing the right locations for sales. Here are the most promising places to sell:

  • Popular Tea Shops and Brands: Partnering with well-known tea brands such as MBA Chai Wala, Chai Sutta Bar, or Mo Chai can ensure steady sales. These brands already have a loyal customer base, and introducing edible cups can enhance their appeal and boost sales.
  • College Campuses: College areas are bustling hubs where tea consumption is exceptionally high. A single tea stall in a college area can sell up to 10,000 cups per day due to the high footfall and the preference for trendy and eco-friendly products among students.
  • Market Areas: Busy market areas are another ideal spot, where tea is a staple for shoppers, vendors, and workers. Selling edible cups here can tap into a large and diverse customer base.
  • Corporate Zones and Offices: Introducing edible cups in corporate areas can cater to professionals who value hygiene, sustainability, and convenience. This could also create opportunities for bulk sales through partnerships with office cafeterias and tea vendors.
By targeting these high-traffic locations, it’s possible to sell up to 10,000 edible tea cups per day, making this a highly profitable and scalable business venture.

  • Raw Material : Available in local market
  • Production Cost per Cup: ₹1.75 to ₹2
  • Selling Price per Cup: ₹3
  • Margin per Cup: ₹1
  • Production Rate: 400 cups per hour
  • Daily Sales: At least 5,000 cups
  • Daily Profit Margin: ₹5,000 to ₹6,000
  • Packaging and Transport Costs: ₹2,000
  • Net Daily Profit: ₹3,000
  • Monthly Income: ₹70,000 to ₹90,000
  • Labor Costs: Only one person needed to handle operations
  • Business Expansion: Additional machines and labor can be added for growth

Note: Calculations may vary based on location and demand. Before proceeding, please verify with your local market to ensure accuracy and feasibility.

Main Drawbacks of Edible Tea Cups :-

  • Product Quality and Customer Satisfaction:

    Challenge: If the edible cups are not of high quality, it can negatively impact customer satisfaction. For example, cups that crack or leak can ruin the experience.
    Solution: Focus on quality control during production. Use durable materials and rigorous testing to ensure cups remain intact when holding hot liquids.

  • Packaging Issues:

    Challenge: Poor packaging can result in breakage during transport, leading to product loss and customer dissatisfaction.
    Solution: Invest in high-quality, protective packaging that prevents damage during transit. Use eco-friendly materials to maintain sustainability.

  • Taste and Flavor:

    Challenge: If the cups lack a good taste or have an unpleasant flavor, customers may not enjoy them, reducing repeat sales.
    Solution: Develop multiple flavors and conduct taste tests with potential customers to ensure wide appeal. Offer options like vanilla, chocolate, or neutral flavors to suit different preferences.

  • Licensing and Compliance:

    Challenge: Selling edible products requires food safety certifications, such as a food license, to comply with local regulations.
    Solution: Obtain the necessary food licenses and ensure that the cups meet all health and safety standards. Display certifications prominently to build customer trust.

  • Expiration Date:

    Challenge: Edible cups have a limited shelf life, and expired products cannot be reused, leading to wastage.
    Solution: Add clear expiration dates on all packages. Implement a supply chain system to manage inventory and ensure products are sold before expiration. Consider offering discounts on near-expiry stock to minimize waste.

Conclusion :-

Edible tea cups offer a unique market opportunity, but success depends on addressing key challenges like product quality, packaging, taste, and regulatory compliance. Ensuring durability, good flavor, proper packaging, and obtaining the necessary licenses will help overcome these obstacles. With proper attention to these factors, edible tea cups can become a valuable and sustainable product in the growing tea industry.

Cow Forming ( गाय पालन ) Cost : ₹1,00,000 - ₹2,00,000

Cow farming is an old and traditional business that continues to be profitable, just like it has been for generations. It offers multiple ways to generate income, primarily through selling milk and milk products. While it may require additional labor to manage the cows and maintain the business, the profits are often high, especially when milk and dairy products are sold at premium rates. With proper care and management, cow farming can be a steady and rewarding source of income.

Demand on market :-

Cow farming has high demand in the market because milk is a basic necessity for everyone, whether it's children, the elderly, or patients. Milk is consumed universally, making it a product that always has a steady market. The great thing about this business is that location is not a constraint—you can sell milk and milk products anywhere. Additionally, milk products like paneer, ghee, and chena are also in high demand, and they can be sold at premium prices, further boosting your income. This makes cow farming a highly profitable venture with diverse revenue streams.

Where to sell :-

The great thing about cow farming is that location is not a constraint—you can sell milk and milk products anywhere. You have several options for selling:
Directly selling milk to customers gives you a steady stream of income with minimal effort. However, the profit margin on milk alone may be lower.
You can also sell milk products like paneer, ghee, and chena, which offer higher profit margins.
To maximize your earnings, you can sell these products directly to shops, hotels, or even dairy shops.
In many villages, there are milk booths where you can sell your milk, making it an easy way to distribute. However, selling milk products to shops or hotels can bring in more income due to the higher selling price of these products.
